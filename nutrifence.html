Nutrifence

An iOS app developed by my team at Archhacks - The University of Washington in St. Louis. As the UX Designer, it was my goal to understand the users and create a product that is both simple and visually engaging.

UX Design
After Effects
Illustrator
48 hours

The Problem

"When I first was diagnosed with Celiac's disease (severe gluten intolerance) almost 10 years ago, a dietician sat down with my mom and I and explained that gluten is a very sneaky ingredient - it can be found in over 100 different forms, from malted barley to couscous to bulgur.

She gave my mom a little dictionary and told her that if she had any doubts about an ingredient, she should look it up and double check to make sure it was okay. Instantly, grocery shopping became a very tedious process of carefully reading lists upon lists upon lists of ingredients. And when I went to a friends' house, it was not uncommon for my mom to send me with gluten-free pasta in order to avoid the panicked phone call that inevitably came when their parents realized they had to figure out whether they had something gluten-free lying around.

So, this weekend we set out to find a solution for people who are in the process of transitioning to a gluten-free lifestyle, as well as an easy way for people unfamiliar with the disease to be able to easily find a safe dining solution."

The Process

I started the design process by learning as much as I can about the problem. I interviewed people with dietary restrictions to understand their frustrations and gather pain points.

Next, I sketched out the wireframes so we can get to prototyping right away. The design and the development had to be worked on simultaneously because of our time constraints, because of this, I was more selective when making design choices.

To communicate my designs better with the developers, I made animations in After Effect to show how I visioned the user flow. Going forward, I probably would not do this again on such time constraint, but I love working with motion, and it's an important aspect of a user's experience.

The Takeaway

Our team won "Best Nutritional Hack" at the Hackathon. This was exciting because it further validated our idea, and we decided to take it further.

Looking back, I have grown tremendously as a designer - I promise I will never make gradients like that ever again. But the most valuable takeaway is working in a multidisciplinary team. I learned how to communicate effectively with developers, I learned my strengths and how I can add value to the team.
