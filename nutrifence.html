App Interface Design


Nutrifence is an iOS app developed by my team and I at ArchHacks held at the University of Washington in St. Louis.

The idea behind the app is simple, just take a picture of an ingredient list on a food product, and based on your personalized dietary needs, it will determine if the product is safe to eat or not. 

As an UX designer, my goal was to design an interface that is intuitive, functional, and plausible over the course of 48 hours.  

The Process 


I always start my design process by determining who my end users are and the problems they are facing. In this case, they are individuals who have recently undergone a dietary change, such as celiac disease. Which makes grocery shopping a very tedious task. 

Next, through rapid prototyping, I sketched out several paths the user can take to reach their end goal. I continued to gather feedback from my team and mentors throughout this process. 

Challenges


One of the design challenges I faced was how to best visually represent the list of ingredients once processed. I quickly learned that users love to get instant feedback from computers. So it was important to also list out the ingredients that the app processed, as well to highlight the unsafe ingredients to create reassurance for the user.

 To further create visual aid, the background colour changes from green for safe ingredients, to red for unsafe ingredients. 

An Extra Touch


I created an animated loading icon using Adobe After Effects and Swift to be implemented into our app. I love working with little details that completes the experience of the user.


Reflections 


Through this experience, I gained more confidence in interface design and solving difficult problems. But most of all, I truly enjoyed working with people from different backgrounds and disciplines. 

We were extremely honoured to win the category of “Best Nutritional Hack” presented by Panera Bread.  

The Next Step...


Seeing the potential, our goal is to have the iOS app released to the app store this year. 

And of course, we love new challenges. The android version is already in the works, as well, we are working to incorporate more dietary options for more user needs. 

My personal goal is to create an updated interface that will appeal to an array of users, and can be implemented with more features.
